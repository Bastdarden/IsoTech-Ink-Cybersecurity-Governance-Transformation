### Cybersecurity Governance Charter

Developed a Cybersecurity Governance Charter establishing the strategic direction, authority structure, decision-making framework, and oversight responsibilities for IsoTech Ink's cybersecurity program.

The charter defines governance objectives, executive sponsorship, stakeholder responsibilities, accountability mechanisms, and reporting structures necessary to align cybersecurity initiatives with business goals and risk management priorities.

This document serves as the foundation for enterprise cybersecurity governance by formalizing leadership involvement, security accountability, and program oversight.

Key Areas Covered:

• Governance Structure
• Executive Sponsorship
• Roles & Responsibilities
• Risk Ownership
• Security Decision-Making
• Policy Oversight
• Compliance Governance
• Security Program Accountability

Frameworks Referenced:

• NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
• NIST SP 800-53
• CIS Controls
• ISO 27001 Governance Principles

### Security Steering Committee Structure

Developed a Security Steering Committee Structure to establish cross-functional cybersecurity governance and executive oversight across the organization.

The committee framework defines committee membership, meeting cadence, reporting responsibilities, decision-making authority, escalation procedures, and risk review processes to support enterprise-wide cybersecurity governance.

The structure was designed to improve communication between business leaders and security stakeholders while ensuring accountability for cybersecurity initiatives and risk management activities.

Key Areas Covered:

• Committee Governance Structure
• Executive Participation
• Department Representation
• Risk Review Procedures
• Security Program Oversight
• Escalation Processes
• Performance Reporting
• Compliance Monitoring

Frameworks Referenced:

• NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
• NIST SP 800-53
• CIS Controls
• ISO 27001 Governance Practices
